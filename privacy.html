1. Scope and definitions

This notice explains how the public Upstate Basketball League website at upstatebasketballleague.com handles information. It applies to public pages such as schedules, standings, team profiles, playoff brackets, league standards, galleries, sponsorship information, and the About page.

In this notice, UBL, we, and our mean the Upstate Basketball League website and the league personnel responsible for operating it. Visitor means anyone who uses the public website. Personal information means information that identifies, relates to, or can reasonably be connected with an individual. Aggregate information means measurements combined across multiple visits or visitors so they are presented as totals, percentages, averages, or trends rather than as an individual's history.

This notice does not replace team, school, event, eligibility, employment, volunteer, or offline league records policies. Restricted coach score-entry tools, administrative spreadsheets, and private gallery-moderation workflows are operational systems with access limited to authorized users.

2. Our privacy principles

UBL uses four principles when making website-data decisions:

  • Choice: optional analytics does not start unless a visitor selects "Allow analytics." Choosing "No thanks" keeps optional analytics disabled.
  • Purpose: information should serve a defined league purpose such as publishing accurate information, improving website performance, understanding combined audience reach, or protecting the service.
  • Restraint: the public analytics implementation should not request names, email addresses, form entries, exact GPS location, academic records, medical information, payment information, or player eligibility information.
  • Accountability: sponsor reporting should use combined measurements. Sponsors should not receive raw visitor logs, visitor-level profiles, or access to an individual's browsing history.
Analytics never makes basketball decisions.

Website activity is not used to determine player eligibility, playing time, team selection, discipline, awards, playoff seeding, or officiating assignments.

4. Essential website activity

The website requests public schedules, scores, standings, team information, and approved gallery images from UBL-managed files and Google services. These requests are necessary to show current league information and do not depend on analytics consent.

The site is delivered through GitHub Pages and may rely on browser, domain, network, and content-delivery services. Those services process standard technical requests needed to return website files, prevent abuse, and maintain availability. UBL may not directly receive or control every technical log created by those providers.

Essential activity includes loading the navigation, retrieving public league data, displaying team logos and approved photographs, remembering basic interface state, resolving website errors, and protecting the website. Rejecting analytics does not block schedules, standings, team pages, bracket information, league standards, or other public content.

5. Optional analytics

After you choose "Allow analytics," the site may use Google Analytics and the UBL private performance feed. If you choose "No thanks," these optional tools do not start. The site also keeps analytics disabled when the browser communicates a supported Do Not Track preference.

The private UBL performance feed

The UBL feed records a limited page-view row containing:

  • The date and time of the measurement.
  • The public page viewed and referring website host.
  • General device class and browser viewport size.
  • Page load time, largest-contentful-paint time, and layout-shift measurement.

The feed accepts only recognized public page names and bounded values. It does not receive a visitor name, email address, UBL account, score-entry identity, advertising identifier, or precise location from the public analytics code. The feed is stored in a private UBL-controlled Google Sheet rather than displayed on the public website.

Google Analytics 4

UBL also uses a Google Analytics 4 property after consent. Google Analytics may use first-party analytics cookies and process information about page activity, sessions, engagement, browser and device characteristics, traffic sources, and coarse geographic areas. Google may use a device's IP address during processing to provide security and general location information; UBL does not configure the public website to send an IP address as a custom analytics field.

UBL currently configures the Google tag with Google Signals disabled, advertising storage denied, advertising user data denied, and advertising personalization denied. UBL does not send a website User-ID, player ID, coach ID, or email address to Google Analytics. Google processes Analytics information under its own terms and privacy documentation. Visitors can review Google's Privacy Policy, Google Analytics privacy controls, and the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on.

What UBL learns

UBL may review totals and trends such as visits, page views, traffic sources, device categories, engagement, popular pages, broad geographic reach, and performance measurements. These reports help answer practical questions: Are families finding the schedule? Is the standings page useful on phones? Did a championship announcement reach the community? Is a large image slowing down a page? Which league resources deserve more attention?

6. Sponsor reporting and league benefits

UBL may use consented analytics to prepare combined audience reports for current or prospective sponsors, community partners, participating programs, or league leadership. The purpose is to demonstrate that the league website is useful, active, and capable of connecting organizations with the UBL community.

Examples of sponsor-facing measurements

  • Total or monthly website visits, active users, sessions, and page views reported in combined form.
  • Combined views of schedules, standings, scores, brackets, team pages, galleries, announcements, and sponsorship pages.
  • Broad device, traffic-source, engagement, and geographic trends when available and appropriate.
  • Combined impressions or clicks for a sponsor placement if UBL later adds a clearly labeled sponsor link or placement measurement.
  • Season-over-season trends used to explain league growth and digital reach.

What sponsors do not receive

UBL does not provide sponsors with raw UBL performance-feed rows, an individual visitor's page history, analytics cookie identifiers, exact locations, private messages, email lists, player contact information, or unrestricted access to the UBL Google Analytics property. A sponsor relationship does not authorize a sponsor to contact a website visitor or build an advertising profile about that visitor.

How this can benefit programs and student-athletes

Responsible audience reporting can help UBL explain its value to potential supporters. Sponsorship revenue or in-kind support may help cover championship awards, event presentation, league operations, officiating or administrative costs, communications, website systems, media resources, and future opportunities that serve participating programs and student-athletes. Analytics helps demonstrate reach; it does not guarantee sponsorship, dictate league spending, or promise a specific benefit to an individual player.

UBL leadership remains responsible for sponsorship decisions, budgets, sponsor recognition, and ensuring that commercial relationships fit the league's mission and standards.

7. Advertising and personalization

Using combined analytics to explain UBL's audience reach to a sponsor is different from using visitor-level information to target advertisements. The current public website does not use Google Analytics for personalized advertising, behavioral remarketing, cross-site advertising profiles, or sponsor-specific visitor lists.

At the time of this notice, the website sends advertising-related Google consent signals as denied and disables Google Signals and ad-personalization signals. If UBL later proposes personalized advertising, remarketing, or another materially different use of visitor-level advertising data, UBL should update this notice and present a separate, clear advertising choice before enabling that use. Permission for ordinary analytics should not automatically become permission for personalized advertising.

UBL may display clearly identified sponsor names, logos, links, acknowledgments, or sponsored content without using personalized tracking. Contextual sponsor recognition based on the page being viewed does not require UBL to build a profile about the visitor.

8. Your choices and controls

Your browser stores "granted" or "denied" under ubl-analytics-consent-v1 in local storage. This value exists to remember the choice on that browser. Choosing "No thanks" prevents optional analytics from loading on future visits from that browser. Clearing browser storage removes the saved choice and may cause the website to ask again.

You can use the Review privacy choices button near the top of this page to reopen the choice panel. A visitor who previously allowed analytics can change the preference to "No thanks." The website then sends a denied consent update when possible and attempts to remove common Google Analytics cookies for the UBL domain.

Browser settings, privacy extensions, cookie controls, network filters, and the Google Analytics opt-out add-on may provide additional controls. Some browsers clear local storage automatically or prevent it from being saved; in that situation the website may not remember the choice between visits.

9. Service providers and sharing

UBL uses service providers to operate the website. Depending on the requested feature and the visitor's consent, those providers may include:

  • GitHub and GitHub Pages for source control, website hosting, deployment, and content delivery.
  • Google Analytics for optional website measurement after consent.
  • Google Apps Script, Sheets, and Drive for public league data feeds, private analytics records, controlled score workflows, and approved gallery assets.
  • Domain, network, email, and browser providers that help visitors reach the website or communicate with UBL.

UBL may share information when reasonably necessary to operate the service, investigate security or misuse, comply with law, respond to lawful process, protect the rights and safety of the league or others, or complete an organizational transition. UBL should limit those disclosures to what is reasonably necessary for the relevant purpose.

Participating teams and representatives supply public league information but do not automatically receive website analytics. Sponsors receive only combined reporting as described above unless a visitor separately and knowingly provides information directly to that sponsor.

10. Students, children, and photographs

UBL serves varsity programs and publishes information that is relevant to student-athletes, families, coaches, officials, and community supporters. The public website is not intended to ask a child under 13 to create an account or submit personal information through public analytics. Visitors under 13 should use the site with a parent or guardian.

UBL does not intentionally use public analytics to create behavioral advertising profiles about student-athletes or children. If UBL learns that personal information from a child under 13 was collected in a way that requires parental notice or consent, UBL should review the circumstances and take appropriate corrective or deletion steps.

Photographs and media

Approved gallery photographs may include student-athletes, coaches, officials, volunteers, families, or spectators. Team-specific uploads are routed through a UBL moderation workflow before publication. Approval of a photograph for league coverage does not authorize sponsors to use that image independently, create advertising profiles from it, or use facial recognition.

UBL should avoid publishing hidden location metadata, private contact information, or captions that unnecessarily identify a minor. A parent, guardian, participant, program representative, or person shown in a photograph may request review or removal by contacting UBL. A request will be evaluated based on identity, authority, league records, safety, legal obligations, and the circumstances of publication.

Scores, rosters, and team information

Game results, standings, schedules, coaches, venues, and team information are supplied or approved for league use. The public website does not currently publish complete player rosters as part of the analytics system. Public league records may be corrected when an authorized representative identifies an error.

11. Communications and submitted information

Email links on the website open the visitor's email provider. The public website does not silently read or collect the message. When someone sends UBL an email, the email provider and UBL receive the sender address, recipients, subject, message, attachments, and related delivery details.

Messages may be used to answer questions, discuss membership or sponsorship, correct league information, review photograph requests, address safety or conduct concerns, and administer league business. Do not send unnecessary sensitive information through ordinary email.

Private coach score-entry and team upload workflows may require a signed-in Google account. Those workflows are not public analytics. Access and submissions are used for the operational purpose presented in the relevant tool.

12. Retention and deletion

UBL keeps information for different periods depending on its purpose:

  • The browser privacy preference remains until it is changed, cleared, or removed by the browser.
  • Google Analytics user-level and event-level retention is governed by the UBL property's administrative setting and Google's service limits and policies.
  • Private UBL performance records may be retained across seasons when reasonably useful for website performance, league growth, sponsor reporting, auditing, or historical comparison. UBL should delete or aggregate records when the underlying detail is no longer reasonably needed.
  • Public schedules, scores, standings, championship results, and league history may be retained as historical league records.
  • Emails and requests may be retained while active and afterward when reasonably needed for follow-up, recordkeeping, safety, dispute resolution, or legal obligations.
  • Approved photographs may remain in league galleries or archives until replaced, removed, or no longer reasonably useful for league documentation.

A request to delete or correct information may require enough detail to identify the relevant record and verify the requester's authority. UBL may retain information when required or permitted for security, legal compliance, historical league records, or the rights of others.

13. Security, external links, and changes

Security

UBL uses measures intended to reduce unnecessary access, including restricted administrative files, allowlisted production hosts, bounded analytics fields, private control sheets, approved gallery workflows, HTTPS delivery, deployment testing, and access through managed service accounts. No website, email system, cloud service, or transmission method can be guaranteed completely secure.

External links

The website may link to maps, team websites, Google services, social platforms, sponsors, or other third parties. Their privacy practices, security, content, and availability are controlled by those organizations. Opening an external link may allow that organization to receive standard request information under its own policy.

Visitors outside New York

UBL is based in New York and the website is intended primarily for the league's Upstate New York community. Internet services may process information in other states or countries. Visitors accessing the site from another jurisdiction are responsible for understanding any local rules that apply to their use, while UBL remains responsible for the practices described in this notice.

Changes to this notice

UBL may update this notice when website features, providers, analytics, sponsorship practices, or legal requirements change. A materially different use of visitor-level information should be disclosed before it begins and, when appropriate, should require a new choice. The updated date below identifies the current public version.

14. Contact UBL

To ask about website data, request a copy of this notice, correct public league information, discuss sponsor reporting, change an analytics preference, or request review of a photograph, email info.upstatebasketballleague@gmail.com.

Include the relevant page, team, photograph, message, or date when possible. Do not send passwords, payment-card details, medical records, or other unnecessary sensitive information.
