Use the WebP files on the website. Matching PNG files are the full-size generated masters retained for future crops or edits. These assets are conceptual brand illustrations, not documentary images of UBL games or participants.

Use `ubl-kingdom-impact-homepage-clean.webp` for the homepage so headings and the UBL mark remain editable, responsive HTML. The campaign lockup is retained only as a reference composite; its text and official UBL mark are baked into the file.

`ubl-kingdom-impact-huddle.webp` is used by `ubl-about.css` as the Core Commitments background. It replaces the previous CGI championship-trophy scene so the inaugural league is not visually presented as having already completed a championship.

`ubl-kingdom-impact-homepage-wide.webp` is the 2400 x 900 desktop homepage hero. `ubl-kingdom-impact-homepage-mobile.webp` is the 768 x 1200 phone treatment. Both use the same text-free Kingdom Impact illustration with intentional navy breathing room, so the browser does not upscale the art and the complete huddle remains readable across responsive crops. The homepage selects between them with a `<picture>` element while keeping the mission text as accessible HTML.

## Shared art direction

- Premium sports-editorial collage with screen-print texture, halftone grain, cut-paper silhouettes, court-line geometry, and restrained arena light.
- Midnight navy, UBL blue, UBL red, white, and a small amount of warm championship light.
- Anonymous figures, blank uniforms, center-safe composition, and consistent camera/collage language across the set.
- No text, UBL logo, team logo, sponsor, jersey number, watermark, photorealistic identity, violent contact, flames, lightning, neon esports effects, or distorted anatomy.

## Prompt set

### Faith / Before the whistle

Use case: `stylized-concept`

Asset type: UBL faith-and-character website illustration.

Primary request: A premium sports-editorial collage of one anonymous varsity basketball athlete standing quietly at center court before a game, holding a basketball low at the side with head slightly bowed. Intersecting arena light and authentic court geometry suggest a subtle cross through negative space rather than displaying a literal religious symbol.

Composition: Wide 3:2 landscape, full figure centered inside a mobile-safe area, large quiet navy space, restrained red/blue balance.

### Competition / Give your best

Use case: `stylized-concept`

Asset type: UBL competition-and-excellence website illustration.

Primary request: A premium sports-editorial collage of two anonymous varsity basketball silhouettes contesting the ball at the peak of a clean athletic play, one carried by deep blue and one by disciplined red, unified by white court arcs and directional halftone movement.

Composition: Wide 3:2 landscape, dynamic diagonal energy with both full silhouettes readable, action centered for responsive crops, intense but respectful.

### Kingdom Impact / Beyond one team

Use case: `stylized-concept`

Asset type: UBL community-and-service website illustration.

Primary request: A premium overhead sports-editorial collage of an anonymous mixed group of athletes, coaches, officials, and community supporters joined shoulder-to-shoulder around a center-court circle. Clean court lines extend outward as connected paths, suggesting influence beyond one team and one result.

Composition: Wide 3:2 landscape, overhead centered circle, clear human silhouettes with simplified hands, generous dark perimeter for responsive crops.

## Shared constraints

Use the supplied UBL logo only as a palette and graphic-weight reference, never as an edit target or an element to reproduce. Use the existing UBL hero art as the texture, court-line, and arena-light reference. Real gallery photography may guide authentic posture or team energy only; do not reproduce identifiable people, uniforms, gym details, or events. Keep the three outputs visibly illustrative and coherent in palette, halftone texture, silhouette language, court geometry, and lighting. No readable text, letters, logos, numbers, watermarks, sponsor marks, real team identities, photorealistic faces, malformed anatomy, or fabricated documentary context.
